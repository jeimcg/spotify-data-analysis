# Spotify Wrapped in July  
**Exploratory Data Analysis and Visualization using Tableau and Python (Matplotlib, NumPy, Pandas, and Seaborn)**

## Project Overview  
In this project, I analyzed my Spotify streaming data from **June 27, 2023 to August 15, 2023** using `.json` and Excel files. The goal was to explore trends and anomalies while visualizing the findings using Tableau and Python libraries like **Matplotlib**, **NumPy**, **Pandas**, and **Seaborn**.

## Findings and Notes  

### Listening Patterns  
- **Peak Listening Days:**  
  - **Wednesdays and Sundays** were the days I listened to music the most.  
    - **Sundays:** Likely due to the "Sunday Scaries" – music helps me manage anxiety as I set goals for the upcoming week.  
    - **Wednesdays:** At the time, I worked as a delivery driver, spending 9-10 hours on the road with music as a companion.

### Unknown Tracks and Artists  
- **Unknown Tracks:**  
  - Many of these tracks are my own music, which I export to my phone and add to Spotify’s local files without metadata.  
- **Unknown Artist:**  
  - Likely to be me again! Over the years, I’ve made music under **10+ pseudonyms** across various genres. This explains why my own music ranks third behind **Lil Darkie** and **Vince Staples**.

### Playlist Insights  
- **Playlist Song Counts:**  
  - Creating bar graphs gave a visual representation of the number of songs in each playlist.  
  - Some playlists are blocked out due to unrecognized fonts or emojis in the titles.  
- **Artist Word Clouds:**  
  - Tableau visuals made it easy to see which artists and albums dominate my playlists.  
  - The word clouds highlight my ongoing love for **2000s and 2010s emo and metalcore** music.

### Data Processing  
- **Excel vs. Python:**  
  - Excel proved to be more intuitive and efficient for pre-processing and cleaning the data compared to using Python alone.
