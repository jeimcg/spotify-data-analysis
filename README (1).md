## Findings and Notes

- During this time, I listened to music the most on Wednesday's and Sunday's. With this newly presented information, I came up with possible answers as to why: One, the Sunday scaries lol. Sometimes I get a bit anxious as I set intentions and goals for the following week so I often use music to self-soothe. It makes sense that I would have more minutes logged for Sunday's than any other day for this reason! On Wednesday, at the time, I was working as a delivery driver and part of what got me through doing that job was music, again. I would drive throughout Western New York for about 9-10 hours a day and then proceed to go home and listen to music, whether actively or passive, until I went to bed.

- Maybe Spotify can start playing more tracks for me that sound like SUNDOWN TOWN by Vince Staples. I failed to mention that I make my own music and that when I bounce tracks out to my phone - and into my local files on Spotify, making them a part of my library - to listen on the go, it doesn't have readily available metadata giving us our Unknown Track! I'm not a fan of inputting that information until it's time to upload said track, finalized, for distribution to stores and streaming services which is far off from the beginning drafts that I often rack up plays with trying to build off of!

- Something tells me again that Unknown Artist is most likely me again lol. I've made music under 10+ pseudonyms spanning many different genres over 12 years! It would make sense that I - and my many pseudonyms - would make up a sizable portion of my most streamed artists, enough to come in third behind Lil Darkie and Vince Staples.

- I know these already simply because I create playlists for fun and the song count within playlists are visible in Spotify's mobile UI but it's nice to have a visual representation using a bar graph of the # of songs in each playlist! The playlist names that are blocked out are playlists with an unrecognized font type or emojis for the title!

- With the Tableau visuals, you can easily and visibly see which artists and/or albums take the spotlight(s) within my playlists simply by their size next to other artists in the cloud! As you can probably tell from my word clouds, I am very much STILL into 2000s and 2010s emo and metalcore music haha!
